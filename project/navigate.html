On The Ground Guide

This section of the website provides info about food, transportation, accommodation, and generally how to navigate your way once you arrive in Ghana.

Transportation

The major means of transport is the trotro (small vans) for short travels and busses for long distance journeys. You may consider booking a flight for long distance travels within the country.

Services like Uber and Bolt operate well within the cities but aren't as effective in small communities and rural areas.

Food

Food in Ghana typically varies depending on what part or region of the country you find yourself. However, the most common are rice, banku, fufuo. Other foods may include bayere ampesi, kokonte also known as abetie.

Accommodation

There are hotels and guest houses. Google Maps works very well in the country so you could look up a few of them.

Currency & Payment

The local currency is the Ghana Cedi (GHS). While cash is king in local markets and for trotros, credit cards are widely accepted in major hotels and supermarkets. Mobile Money (MoMo) is also incredibly popular for daily transactions.

Visa Requirements

Most travelers require a visa to enter Ghana, which should be secured online or from a Ghanaian embassy before arrival. Ensure your passport is valid for at least six months beyond your departure date and that you have a valid Yellow Fever vaccination certificate.

Local Customs & Etiquette

Ghanaians are known for their exceptional hospitality. Always greet people with your right hand, as using the left hand for eating, greeting, or giving/receiving items is culturally considered impolite.

Safety & Emergency Contacts

Ghana is generally a very safe and peaceful country for travelers. However, exercise standard precautions: avoid walking alone in unlit areas at night and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded markets.

Emergency Numbers:

  • Police: 191
  • Fire Service: 192
  • Ambulance: 193

Language Basics

English is the official language, but you will hear many local languages. Learning a few words of Twi will instantly win you smiles:

  • Akwaaba – Welcome
  • Medaase – Thank you
  • Eti sen? – How is it going?
  • Chale – Friend/Dude (slang)

SIM Cards & Internet

You can easily purchase a local SIM card at the airport or local kiosks. MTN and Vodafone (Telecel) offer extensive coverage. You will need your passport to register the SIM card legally upon purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the tap water safe to drink?

It is highly recommended to drink bottled or filtered "sachet" water rather than municipal tap water.

When is the best time to visit?

The dry season from November to March is generally preferred, as humidity is lower and roads are easier to travel.
